# E-Commerce Haircare: Customer Cohort & Product Mix Analysis
A data-driven analysis of a DTC haircare brand decoding customer behavior to optimize marketing spend and improve retention loops. 

## Key Business Insights
**The Northeast Friction Point**:Customers in the Northeast take **3.8 days to convert** compared to an average of 2.8 days in other regions—highlighting a major retention opportunity.
**Regional Drivers:** The highest concentration of High-Value Customers (HVCs) is heavily localized in **Texas, California, Illinois, Georgia, and New York**.
**High-Value Product Mix:** Top-tier customer baskets are overwhelmingly dominated by core treatment categories like **Shampoos + Conditioners** and **Hair Masks + Scalp Treatments**.
**Campaign Efficiency:** Specialized acquisition structures (**_TM_**) scale effectively for high-value cohorts, while standard **Pmax** campaigns bring varied, lower-value results.

## Recommendations
1. Allocate targeted retargeting spend to the **Northeast** to accelerate the 3.8-day purchase cycle.
2. Build automated cross-sell bundles pairing core **Shampoos** with high-margin **Hair Masks**.
3. Scale performance budgets upward for high-intent campaign funnels over raw traffic volume.
4. Use Dry Shampoo as entry point and re-target the users after 30-days with shampoo and conditioner sets and increase their LTV using bundles found from product mix.
